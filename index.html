What is viruses?

???

A type of malware that can replicate itself and spread to other computers. Viruses can damage or delete files, modify applications, or disable system functions. They can enter a computer through downloads, attachments, or be hidden in free-trial software.

What is malware?

???

A general term for any malicious software, including viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, adware, and ransomware. Malware can be distributed through infected websites, flash drives, emails, and other means.

What is spam?

???

Unwanted or unsolicited emails, typically sent in large quantities. Spammers use spam emails to advertise products or services, or to spread malware and viruses.

Spam is typically sent for commercial purposes, such as advertising, phishing, or spreading malware. For example, you might receive a spam email from a cruise company offering deals, even if you've never signed up for their emails. Or, you might receive a phishing email that appears to come from your bank or insurance company, but is actually an attempt to steal your information.

Antivirus Software?

???

A computer program that prevents, detects, and removes malware. Antivirus software scans files and incoming email for viruses, and deletes anything malicious. It can also protect against spam, malicious URLs, and phishing.

What is plagiarism?

Plagiarism means using someone else's work without giving them proper credit. In academic writing, plagiarizing involves using words, ideas, or information from a source without citing it correctly. In the Philippines, plagiarism is the same as copyright and could be consider under the cybercrime law of the Republic Act 10175, and according to the Department of Justice, plagiarism is not a crime but it is the same in copyright violation. hj

Cases of plagiarism

Jayson Blair In 2003, Blair was exposed for plagiarizing and inventing facts in many of his stories for the New York Times. He resigned from the paper, and his plagiarism led to top editors leaving and a focus on editorial oversight and journalism ethics.

Consequent of plagiarism

Plagiarism is considered a breach of academic or work ethics and can have serious consequences. These can include: Suspension or expulsion, Damage to your reputation, Loss of research funding or job, and Legal consequences for copyright infringement.
